
CREDIT RISK MANAGEMENT POLICY

Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Document Version: 3.0
Regulatory Framework: CBN Prudential Guidelines, Basel III

1. PURPOSE
This policy establishes credit risk management framework in compliance with Central Bank of Nigeria (CBN) prudential guidelines and international best practices.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all lending activities including corporate loans, SME lending, retail credit, and trade finance.

3. CREDIT APPROVAL LIMITS

3.1 Individual Lending Limits
- Up to ₦10,000,000: Branch Manager + Credit Officer
- ₦10,000,001 - ₦50,000,000: Regional Credit Manager
- ₦50,000,001 - ₦200,000,000: Head of Credit + CFO
- ₦200,000,001 - ₦1,000,000,000: Executive Credit Committee
- Above ₦1,000,000,000: Board Credit Committee

3.2 Single Obligor Limit
Maximum exposure to single borrower: 20% of shareholders' funds (per CBN regulation)

3.3 Large Exposures
Exposures above ₦500,000,000 require Board notification within 48 hours

4. CREDIT ASSESSMENT CRITERIA

4.1 Minimum Requirements
- Credit bureau check (CRC report mandatory)
- Financial statements (audited for corporate, 2 years minimum)
- Collateral valuation by approved valuers
- Cash flow analysis and debt service coverage ratio ≥ 1.25
- Management assessment for corporate borrowers

4.2 Prohibited Lending
- Insider lending above 10% of shareholders' funds
- Lending to entities on CBN blacklist
- Unsecured lending above ₦5,000,000 without Executive approval

5. COLLATERAL REQUIREMENTS

5.1 Acceptable Collateral
- Real estate (maximum LTV 60%)
- Cash collateral and fixed deposits (100% coverage)
- Quoted equity securities (maximum LTV 40%)
- Bank guarantees from rated institutions
- Domiciliation of cash flows

5.2 Collateral Valuation
- Real estate: Revaluation every 2 years
- Securities: Market-to-market monthly
- All valuations by CBN-approved valuers

6. NON-PERFORMING LOANS (NPL)

6.1 Classification
- Past due 1-90 days: Watchlist
- Past due 91-180 days: Substandard
- Past due 181-360 days: Doubtful
- Past due >360 days: Lost

6.2 Provisioning (IFRS 9 Compliant)
- Stage 1 (Performing): 1% provision
- Stage 2 (Underperforming): 5% provision
- Stage 3 (Non-performing): 50-100% provision

6.3 NPL Management
- NPL ratio target: Below 5% (CBN requirement)
- Monthly NPL review committee
- Recovery unit activation for loans past due >90 days

7. CREDIT CONCENTRATION

7.1 Sector Limits
- Oil & Gas: Maximum 25% of total loan book
- Real Estate & Construction: Maximum 20%
- Manufacturing: Maximum 15%
- SME: Minimum 10% (CBN directive)

7.2 Geographic Concentration
Maximum 40% exposure in any single state

8. DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS
- Loan agreement (reviewed by legal)
- Memorandum of deposit for collateral
- Post-dated cheques (where applicable)
- Personal/Corporate guarantee
- Board resolution (for corporate borrowers)

9. REPORTING
- Monthly credit risk report to ALCO
- Quarterly Board report on credit portfolio
- Annual ICAAP submission to CBN
- Daily large exposure reporting to CBN

10. REVIEW AND COMPLIANCE
Annual policy review by Board Risk Committee
Internal audit quarterly review of compliance
