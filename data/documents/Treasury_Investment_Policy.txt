
TREASURY AND INVESTMENT POLICY

Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Document Version: 2.5
Regulatory Framework: CBN Guidelines, Basel III Liquidity Standards

1. PURPOSE
Govern treasury operations and investment activities to ensure liquidity, safety, and optimal returns while maintaining CBN compliance.

2. INVESTMENT OBJECTIVES (Priority Order)
2.1 Liquidity - Maintain adequate liquidity for operations
2.2 Safety - Capital preservation
2.3 Compliance - Meet all CBN requirements
2.4 Returns - Maximize returns within acceptable risk

3. LIQUIDITY REQUIREMENTS

3.1 CBN Mandated Ratios
- Liquidity Ratio: Minimum 30% of total deposits
- Cash Reserve Ratio (CRR): As specified by CBN (currently 32.5%)
- Loan-to-Deposit Ratio: Minimum 65% (CBN directive)

3.2 Internal Limits
- Maintain minimum ₦50 billion in liquid assets
- Maximum 80% LDR (internal limit below CBN max of 100%)

4. AUTHORIZED INVESTMENTS

4.1 Permitted Instruments
- Central Bank of Nigeria Treasury Bills
- Federal Government of Nigeria Bonds
- CBN Open Market Operations (OMO)
- Bank placements (with rated institutions only)
- Nigerian Treasury Bonds (NTBs)
- State Government Bonds (AAA rated only)
- Bankers Acceptances
- Commercial Papers (A1 rating minimum)

4.2 Prohibited Investments
- Equity securities (except strategic investments with Board approval)
- Real estate (except bank's own use)
- Commodities and derivatives (except CBN-approved hedging)
- Foreign currency speculation
- Cryptocurrency and digital assets
- Unrated corporate bonds

5. INVESTMENT LIMITS

5.1 Concentration Limits
- Maximum 40% in FGN securities
- Maximum 25% in CBN OMO
- Maximum 20% in bank placements
- Maximum 10% with any single counterparty (except CBN/FGN)
- Maximum 15% in corporate instruments

5.2 Maturity Limits
- Average portfolio maturity: Maximum 180 days
- No single investment >1 year (except FGN bonds)
- Minimum 15% in overnight/next-day instruments

6. FOREIGN EXCHANGE MANAGEMENT

6.1 FX Position Limits
- Net Open Position (NOP): Maximum 1% of shareholders' funds (per CBN)
- Overnight NOP: Maximum 0.5% of shareholders' funds
- Foreign currency assets must not exceed 125% of foreign currency liabilities

6.2 FX Risk Management
- Daily monitoring of FX exposures
- Monthly FX committee review
- Hedging permitted only through CBN-authorized instruments
- FX forward contracts require CFO approval

7. CREDIT RATINGS REQUIREMENTS

7.1 Minimum Ratings for Investments
- Bank Placements: Fitch/S&P A- or equivalent
- Corporate Bonds: A rating minimum
- State Bonds: AAA rating required
- Commercial Paper: A1 rating required

7.2 Rating Monitoring
- Quarterly review of counterparty ratings
- Immediate exit if rating falls below minimum
- Monthly credit committee review

8. APPROVAL AUTHORITY

8.1 Investment Transactions
- Up to ₦5 billion: Treasurer
- ₦5 - ₦20 billion: Head of Treasury + CFO
- ₦20 - ₦50 billion: ALCO approval
- Above ₦50 billion: Board Finance Committee

8.2 New Investment Categories
Any investment type not listed requires Board approval

9. REPORTING REQUIREMENTS

9.1 Internal Reporting
- Daily liquidity position to CFO
- Weekly treasury report to ALCO
- Monthly performance report to Board
- Quarterly compliance certification

9.2 Regulatory Reporting
- Daily returns to CBN (liquidity, CRR, NOP)
- Weekly sectoral allocation report
- Monthly balance sheet returns
- Quarterly ICAAP submission

10. OPERATIONAL CONTROLS
- Segregation of front office, middle office, back office
- Dual approval for all transactions >₦1 billion
- Daily mark-to-market valuation
- Independent price verification
- Monthly reconciliation with CBN records
