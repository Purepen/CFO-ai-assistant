
CAPITAL EXPENDITURE POLICY

Effective Date: January 1, 2024
Document Version: 2.0

1. PURPOSE
Establish guidelines for capital expenditure (CAPEX) planning, approval, and monitoring to ensure optimal resource allocation and regulatory compliance.

2. SCOPE
Applies to all capital expenditures including:
- Branch expansion and renovation
- IT infrastructure and software
- Furniture, fixtures, and equipment
- Building and property acquisitions
- ATM and digital banking infrastructure

3. DEFINITION OF CAPITAL EXPENDITURE
Any expenditure >₦500,000 with useful life >1 year that:
- Enhances asset value
- Extends asset life
- Improves operating efficiency

4. APPROVAL THRESHOLDS

4.1 Standard CAPEX
- Up to ₦5,000,000: Department Head + Procurement
- ₦5,000,001 - ₦25,000,000: Divisional Head + CFO
- ₦25,000,001 - ₦100,000,000: Executive Committee (EXCO)
- ₦100,000,001 - ₦500,000,000: Managing Director + CFO
- Above ₦500,000,000: Board of Directors

4.2 Branch Expansion/New Branches
- Branch renovation: As per standard CAPEX above
- New branch (greenfield): Board approval mandatory
- Branch relocation: EXCO approval
- Estimated cost per new branch: ₦80-150 million

4.3 IT Infrastructure
- Core banking upgrades: Board approval
- Digital banking platforms: EXCO approval
- Software licenses <₦10 million: IT Head + CFO
- Cybersecurity investments: Expedited approval process

5. CAPEX BUDGETING

5.1 Annual Budget Process
- CAPEX budget prepared by October (for next year)
- Departmental submissions by September 30
- CFO consolidation and review
- Board approval by December 15

5.2 Budget Allocation
- Branch network: 35-40% of total CAPEX
- IT and digital banking: 30-35%
- Head office infrastructure: 10-15%
- ATM and cash management: 10-15%
- Others: 5-10%

5.3 Budget Monitoring
- Monthly variance reports
- Quarterly reforecast
- Any item exceeding budget by >10% requires re-approval

6. JUSTIFICATION REQUIREMENTS

6.1 Mandatory Documentation
- Business case (including ROI analysis)
- At least 3 vendor quotations
- Technical specifications
- Implementation timeline
- Risk assessment
- Environmental impact assessment (for buildings)

6.2 ROI Requirements
- Minimum IRR: 15% (or cost of capital + 3%)
- Payback period: Maximum 5 years (3 years for IT projects)
- NPV must be positive
- Strategic projects may have exception with Board approval

7. VENDOR SELECTION

7.1 Procurement Process
- >₦10 million: Formal tender process
- >₦50 million: Open competitive bidding
- Single source: Requires justification and CFO approval
- Related party vendors: Board approval mandatory

7.2 Vendor Requirements
- Financial stability assessment
- Technical capability evaluation
- Reference checks
- Compliance with local content requirements

8. PROJECT IMPLEMENTATION

8.1 Project Management
- Project sponsor assigned (senior management)
- Project manager from relevant department
- Monthly steering committee meetings
- Quarterly Board updates for major projects

8.2 Payment Terms
- Maximum 30% advance payment (with bank guarantee)
- Milestone-based payments
- 10% retention until warranty period ends
- Final payment upon satisfactory completion

9. ASSET CAPITALIZATION

9.1 Capitalization Criteria
- Asset cost includes: Purchase price, installation, shipping, taxes
- Capitalize when asset is ready for use
- Depreciation starts when asset is commissioned

9.2 Depreciation Rates
- Buildings: 2% per annum (50 years)
- IT equipment: 25% per annum (4 years)
- Furniture & fixtures: 10% per annum (10 years)
- Motor vehicles: 20% per annum (5 years)
- ATMs: 20% per annum (5 years)

10. POST-IMPLEMENTATION REVIEW
- PIR conducted within 6 months of project completion
- Actual vs. projected benefits comparison
- Lessons learned documentation
- Findings presented to Board for projects >₦100 million

11. DISPOSAL OF ASSETS

11.1 Disposal Approval
- Up to ₦5 million: CFO
- ₦5-25 million: EXCO
- Above ₦25 million: Board

11.2 Disposal Process
- Open bidding for assets >₦5 million
- Fair market value assessment
- Proceeds credited to General Reserve
